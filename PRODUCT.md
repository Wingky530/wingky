# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Broad audience: employers and recruiters evaluating craft skills, fellow developers exploring open-source work, and potential clients seeking collaboration. All arrive expecting a portfolio that demonstrates the quality it advertises.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio and landing page for Wingky — a "vibe coder who actually gives a damn." The site itself is the portfolio's primary exhibit: every animation, interaction, and layout choice demonstrates the craft, motion design, and attention to detail that define the brand.

## Brand Personality

Craft-conscious, dark, precise. Meticulous and anti-slop — every element earns its place. The portfolio feels hand-made, intentional, and unmistakably human-authored. Motion is purposeful, not decorative.

## Anti-references

Must avoid all forms of AI-slop design patterns. No gradient text, no glassmorphism, no generic card grids with icon+heading+body, no numbered section markers (01/02/03), no tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, no hero-metric templates, no side-stripe borders as accent. The portfolio must never read as templated or AI-generated.

## Design Principles

1. **Craft over convention** — Every element earns its place. No defaults, no AI reflexes, no patterns that exist only because "landing pages do this."
2. **Motion with purpose** — Animation reveals structure and state. It never decorates for its own sake.
3. **Dark and precise** — Dark is the default; light is the alternate. Both are equally considered, contrast-verified, and intentional.
4. **Anti-slop rigor** — Every design decision is checked against the question: "Would an AI have done this?" If yes, rewrite.
5. **Show, don't tell** — The portfolio itself demonstrates the skill and craft it advertises.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard best practices: reduced-motion support, keyboard-navigable interactions, sufficient contrast. No strict WCAG level target, but body text contrast must remain readable.
